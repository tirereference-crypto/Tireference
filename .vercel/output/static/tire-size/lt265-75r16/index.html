LT265/75R16 is a truck/SUV-oriented size that pairs 265 mm width with a 75% sidewall on an 16" rim. At roughly 31.6" overall (~7.8" sidewall), it is typically chosen for clearance, load capacity, and mixed on/off-road use rather than max fuel economy. Products indexed for this size include all-terrain, highway-terrain, and winter options depending on actual coverage. Expect heavier steering and a possible speedometer change vs shorter factory sizes — verify clearance and load range before upgrading.

  • Strong fit for trucks/SUVs, towing, gravel, trails, or overland use depending on tread choice.
  • Indexed products include 2 brands and 12 full-spec rows.
  • Tradeoffs may include ride comfort, steering weight, clearance, speedometer change, or fuel economy depending on the jump from stock.

FAQ

Is LT265/75R16 good for off-road and truck use?

LT265/75R16 is a capable off-road and truck use size when clearance, load capacity, or mixed trail use matter. At roughly 31.65" overall (~7.82" sidewall on an 16" rim), it sits in the truck/SUV capability band rather than a pure fuel-economy size. Pair it with the right tread and load range for your mileage mix.

What vehicles use LT265/75R16 tires?

LT265/75R16 is commonly seen on or upgraded to by owners of trucks, SUVs, and crossover platforms that can clear this diameter. Exact factory fitment depends on trim, wheel offset, and suspension height — confirm clearance and load requirements before treating it as a direct replacement.

What load ranges are available?

Indexed products for LT265/75R16 include load ranges such as E and D, depending on brand and model. Load capacity still comes from the load index and inflation table on the specific tire — not the size code alone. Match the load range to your GVWR and intended payload or towing use.

Which brands make LT265/75R16 tires?

Indexed master-database coverage for LT265/75R16 includes BFGoodrich and Toyo. Availability still varies by load range, tread type, and season — check the product cards and full-spec table on this page for models currently listed.

How does LT265/75R16 compare to 285/75R16?

LT265/75R16 measures about 31.65" overall versus 32.83" for 285/75R16 (-1.2" / -3.6%). The shorter size usually trims rotational mass and can raise the speedometer reading relative to actual speed, with less ground clearance. Always check fender clearance and load rating before switching.

What is the load capacity of LT265/75R16?

Load capacity depends on the exact tire, load index, and inflation pressure — not the size code alone. Check the manufacturer load table and your vehicle placard when setting pressure or carrying heavy loads. If multiple load ranges exist for this size, choose the one that meets or exceeds the placard requirement.
